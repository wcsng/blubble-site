Pre-Requisites

You will need the following devices to collect data for our experiment.

1.6 - 8 meters of space where you can walk around and collect the data.

2.Two Devices with Bluetooth capability

a.One iPhone or an Android-based phone where you can install the app ( this device will be the receiver)​

b.For the second device, you can use any Bluetooth enabled device (this

will be the transmitter​ )​. However, if you have a second android device, we recommend you to use that as the transmitter because we can get more data points from Android for our algorithm

Experiments and Setup:

The following experiment will help us understand how Bluetooth signal values can be used to detect distance between devices. In particular, we are looking to classify if another device is within 6 ft of you.

1. First, set up the transmitter​ ​described in Installation​ ​and begin transmitting the beacon. Place it on the floor or some furniture.

2.Next, start the experiment with both devices very close to each other. Press

Start Scan

3.Walk at a comfortable pace within this radius for approximately 1 minute.

4.Next, toggle the Inside​ 6ft/Outside 6ft​as​ you walk far enough away from the transmitter​device.

5.Again, continue to walk outside this radius (up to a distance of 15 ft) for 1 minute.

6.Tap Stop​ Scan​and​ Upload​ Data.​

7.Repeat the experiment with the phone in the pocket. Toggle the In​ Pocket

switch, tap Start Scan and repeat steps (1)-(5). For the In​ Pocket testing,​ you can turn the screen off and place the device in your pocket after toggling the appropriate switches. After walking roughly 6ft distance, you can take the phone out of your pocket and toggle the 6ft switch.

8.Repeat step (1)-(6) for another environment in your home.

**Important: Please remember to toggle in Inside​ 6 ft / Outside 6 ft​when crossing the imaginary 6 ft boundary from the transmitter device **

Continue to the next section to install the necessary applications on your devices.

Testing

iPhone/iPad Receiver:

Once you have the app, you can directly get to data collection.

Note:​You will start within​ a 2m radius​to the transmitter.

1.On an iOS transmitter​ ​device, turn on the Bluetooth.

2.Open the app BLEScanRSSI app.

3.The following screen will populate with multiple Bluetooth devices in the surrounding.

a.iOS​transmitters​ ​show up with its name.

b.Android​transmitters​ ​show up with their device name

c.Linux​transmitters​ :​show up with their device name

d.Windows​transmitters:​​support not available

4.Select the device you want to use as a transmitter​ .​

5.Toggling the In​ Hand/In pocket​button​ indicates data was collected with the receiver in the pocket.

6.Click on the​Start​ Scan​button​ to collect the RSSI values.

7.Toggling Inside​ 6ft/Outside 6ft​switch indicates if you’re farther than 6 ft from the transmitter.

8.Once the data has been collected as per instructions, tap the Stop​ Scan button.

9.Press Store​ data​if​ the data collection was successful.

10.Press Reset​ ​if the data collection wasn’t successful or you make a mistake and restart the process.

Android Receiver

1.Open the BLEScanRSSI app and allow all permissions.

2.On an Android​ ​transmitter​ ​device,

a.tap Transmit​ Beacons.​

b.First, tap Save​ Phone Data to​ upload the transmitter​ 's​ device information to Cloud. This will generate a QR Code which will be needed in the next steps.

c.Toggle the switch to begin transmitting the beacons.

3.On an iOS​ transmitter​ ​device,

a.Click on the Advertise​ ​button at the bottom of the screen.

b.Toggle the BLE device switch.

4.On the receiver​ ​device, tap Scan​ For Beacons

5.The following screen should populate the drop down menu with multiple beaconing devices in the surroundings.

a.iOS​transmitters​ ​show up with the name "BLE​ device"​

b.Android​transmitters​ ​show up with their device name

c.Linux​transmitters​ :​show up with their device name

d.Windows​transmitters:​​support not yet available

6.Select the device and tap Start​ Collecting RSSI From Device

7.Tap the Scan​ ​button​ to collect the RSSI values.

8.Toggling More​ Than 6ft​switch​ indicates if you’re farther than 6 ft from the transmitter.​

9.Toggling the​In​ Pocket​indicates​ data was collected with the receiver in the pocket.

10.Once the data has been collected as per instructions, tap Stop​ Scanning​and Upload Data.​

11.In the dialog box, tap No​ if​ you did not generate a QR Code and proceed to the next step. Tap Yes​ if​ you generated a QR Code and continue scanning the QR Code.

12.In the dialog box, tap Yes​ to​ collect more data with the current experimental setup or No​ to​ exit to the main menu.
