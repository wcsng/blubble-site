Vision

BluBLE is an initiative by researchers at WCSNG Lab UCSD, to help communities in the COVID-19 pandemic crisis. We have been leading the research efforts in harnessing wireless signals to sense the environment and localize human beings in it. The aspect of localization of human beings in an environment could help out the communities in effectively enforcing unforeseen protocols like social distancing, self-quarantining & contact tracing, which are at the frontlines in this fight against the COVID-19 pandemic.

COVID-19 has been a rapidly evolving pandemic owing to its two week incubation period and high communicability. Various international health agencies have touted the merits of social distancing, strict lockdowns have resulted in a decline of new cases. Lockdowns do help social distancing, but it has had a drastic effect on economic and social conditions of people across the world.Hence, to again open up our streets and public spaces, we need to be able to have:

  • A ubiquitous system that alerts you if someone comes closer than 6 ft to you or warns about specific indoor spaces having a higher likelihood of COVID-19
  • A private and voluntary system which will effectively anonymize contact tracking and location tagging.

How do wireless signals help in achieving the above points? Wireless signals get weaker as they travel more distance over the air. You would've observed this with your Wi-Fi router, the closer you go to it the better signal you get. This same phenomena, applied to Bluetooth wireless signals emitted by smartphones, which have almost become ubiquitous across the entire world today, could be used to infer if there is another person within 6 ft from you. Furthermore, what if we can make all the people in the environment collaborate, with help of wireless signals exchanged between them, in order to perform private and voluntary localization of a sick person nearby? These are some of the approaches BluBLE is exploring in order to better equip humanity in this fight against COVID-19.
